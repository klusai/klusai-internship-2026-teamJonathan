================================================================================
STREAMING SERVICE CHURN ANALYSIS - EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

ANALYSIS DATE: 2024
DATASET: 500 customers
OVERALL CHURN RATE: 38.6% (193 churned, 307 retained)

================================================================================
METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

1. Statistical Analysis: T-tests to identify significant differences between 
   churned and retained customers across all numerical features

2. Machine Learning: Random Forest Classifier to quantify feature importance
   and rank the relative impact of each variable on churn prediction

3. Categorical Analysis: Churn rate comparison across subscription tiers,
   genres, and other categorical variables

4. All numerical features showed HIGHLY SIGNIFICANT differences (p < 0.001)
   between churned and retained customers

================================================================================
TOP 7 CHURN DRIVERS (RANKED BY IMPORTANCE)
================================================================================

1. TOTAL VIEWING HOURS [Importance: 0.254] ★★★★★
   ----------------------------------------------------------------
   - MOST IMPORTANT PREDICTOR OF CHURN
   - Churned customers: 66.6 hours/month (avg)
   - Retained customers: 83.2 hours/month (avg)
   - Difference: -16.6 hours/month (-20% lower engagement)
   - Statistical significance: p < 0.001 (highly significant)
   
   INSIGHT: Low viewing time is the single strongest churn signal.
   Customers who don't engage with content are at highest risk.

2. NUMBER OF UNIQUE TITLES WATCHED [Importance: 0.171] ★★★★
   ----------------------------------------------------------------
   - SECOND MOST IMPORTANT FACTOR
   - Churned customers: 19.5 unique titles/month (avg)
   - Retained customers: 23.7 unique titles/month (avg)
   - Difference: -4.3 titles/month (-18% less variety)
   - Statistical significance: p < 0.001 (highly significant)
   
   INSIGHT: Content exploration matters. Users who sample diverse
   content find more value and are more likely to stay.

3. AVERAGE SESSION DURATION [Importance: 0.168] ★★★★
   ----------------------------------------------------------------
   - THIRD MOST IMPORTANT FACTOR
   - Churned customers: 49.4 minutes/session (avg)
   - Retained customers: 57.8 minutes/session (avg)
   - Difference: -8.3 minutes/session (-14% shorter)
   - Statistical significance: p < 0.001 (highly significant)
   
   INSIGHT: Shorter sessions indicate lower engagement per viewing.
   Users who are deeply engaged watch longer.

4. CUSTOMER SERVICE INTERACTIONS [Importance: 0.123] ★★★
   ----------------------------------------------------------------
   - FOURTH MOST IMPORTANT FACTOR
   - Churned customers: 3.18 interactions/year (avg)
   - Retained customers: 2.49 interactions/year (avg)
   - Difference: +0.69 interactions/year (+28% more contact)
   - Statistical significance: p < 0.001 (highly significant)
   
   INSIGHT: More customer service contact = more problems.
   High CS interaction is a warning sign of dissatisfaction.
   Churn rate increases significantly with 3+ interactions.

5. BINGE-WATCHING SESSIONS [Importance: 0.109] ★★★
   ----------------------------------------------------------------
   - Churned customers: 6.2 binge sessions/month (avg)
   - Retained customers: 7.7 binge sessions/month (avg)
   - Difference: -1.5 sessions/month (-20% fewer)
   - Statistical significance: p < 0.001 (highly significant)
   
   INSIGHT: Binge-watching indicates strong content satisfaction.
   Users who binge-watch have found compelling content.

6. CONTENT GENRE PREFERENCE [Importance: 0.103] ★★
   ----------------------------------------------------------------
   Churn rates by genre (highest to lowest):
   - Horror: 52.3% churn rate (HIGH RISK)
   - Thriller: 48.3% churn rate (HIGH RISK)
   - Action: 44.6% churn rate (ABOVE AVERAGE)
   - Romance: 41.8% churn rate (ABOVE AVERAGE)
   - SciFi: 40.5% churn rate (ABOVE AVERAGE)
   - Standard: 39.5% churn rate (AVERAGE)
   - Drama: 35.3% churn rate (BELOW AVERAGE)
   - Comedy: 33.0% churn rate (BELOW AVERAGE)
   - Documentary: 25.9% churn rate (LOW RISK)
   
   INSIGHT: Genre preference correlates with retention.
   Documentary, Comedy, and Drama viewers are more loyal.

7. MONTHLY COST / SUBSCRIPTION TIER [Importance: 0.048 + 0.026] ★
   ----------------------------------------------------------------
   Churn rates by tier:
   - Basic ($7.99): 43.5% churn rate (HIGHEST)
   - Standard ($12.99): 39.5% churn rate
   - Premium ($17.99): 24.1% churn rate (LOWEST)
   
   Average monthly cost:
   - Churned customers: $11.18
   - Retained customers: $12.11
   - Statistical significance: p < 0.01 (significant)
   
   INSIGHT: Premium subscribers are more loyal, possibly due to
   higher commitment or better features. However, subscription
   tier is LESS important than engagement metrics.

================================================================================
KEY PATTERNS IDENTIFIED
================================================================================

THE LOW ENGAGEMENT PROFILE (High Churn Risk):
✗ Watches < 70 hours/month
✗ Explores < 20 unique titles/month
✗ Average session < 50 minutes
✗ Has 3+ customer service interactions/year
✗ Basic tier subscription
✗ Prefers Horror/Thriller genres

THE HIGH RETENTION PROFILE (Low Churn Risk):
✓ Watches > 80 hours/month
✓ Explores > 23 unique titles/month
✓ Average session > 55 minutes
✓ Has ≤ 2 customer service interactions/year
✓ Premium tier subscription
✓ Prefers Documentary/Comedy/Drama genres

BUSINESS RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (High Impact):

1. ENGAGEMENT MONITORING SYSTEM
   → Create real-time alerts for users whose viewing hours drop below
     70 hours/month or show declining trend
   → Implement automated outreach campaign for low-engagement users

2. CONTENT DISCOVERY INITIATIVES
   → Improve recommendation algorithms to increase title variety
   → Create personalized content discovery features
   → Highlight diverse content in email campaigns
   → Gamify content exploration (badges for trying new genres)

3. SESSION EXTENSION STRATEGIES
   → Optimize autoplay and "next episode" features
   → Reduce friction in content continuation
   → Send "continue watching" reminders
   → Test binge-friendly content releases

4. CUSTOMER SERVICE INTERVENTION
   → Flag accounts with 3+ CS interactions as high-risk
   → Proactive outreach to resolve recurring issues
   → Root cause analysis of most common complaints
   → Consider special retention offers for high-CS-contact users

5. TIER MIGRATION STRATEGY
   → Incentivize Basic users to upgrade to Standard/Premium
   → Free trial of Premium features for at-risk Basic users
   → Highlight exclusive Premium content

CONTENT STRATEGY:

6. GENRE-SPECIFIC RETENTION
   → Increase Documentary, Comedy, and Drama content (low churn genres)
   → Improve quality/quantity of Horror and Thriller content
   → Cross-promote stable genres to high-risk genre viewers

7. BINGE-WORTHY CONTENT
   → Prioritize series-based content over standalone titles
   → Release complete seasons (not weekly episodes) where appropriate
   → Create compelling multi-episode narratives

PREDICTIVE ANALYTICS:

8. CHURN PREDICTION MODEL
   → Deploy the Random Forest model (or similar) in production
   → Score all users monthly for churn risk
   → Target top 10% highest-risk users with retention campaigns
   → A/B test different intervention strategies

9. EARLY WARNING SYSTEM
   → Monitor the 4-week trend in viewing hours
   → Alert on 30%+ decrease in engagement
   → Track content variety consumption patterns

================================================================================
CONCLUSION
================================================================================

Churn in this streaming service is primarily driven by LOW ENGAGEMENT rather
than price sensitivity or service tier. The data clearly shows that customers
leave because they're not finding enough value in the content, not because
of cost concerns.

The three engagement metrics (viewing hours, content variety, session duration)
together account for nearly 60% of the predictive power for churn. This means
retention efforts should focus primarily on:

1. Getting users to watch more content
2. Helping users discover diverse titles they'll enjoy
3. Creating compelling content that keeps users engaged longer

Customer service interactions serve as an early warning system - high contact
frequency indicates problems that, if unresolved, lead to churn.

Subscription tier matters, but is a secondary factor. Even Premium users will
churn if engagement is low, and Basic users will stay if engagement is high.

The path to reducing churn is clear: INCREASE ENGAGEMENT through better
content, better discovery, and proactive issue resolution.
